Vaibhav Agarwal

Nationality: Indian | Date of Birth: 21/02/2002

Current Role: 1st year Doctoral Researcher at AGAPP–TUS7AGP

Hello, I’m Vaibhav Agarwal, a passionate and hard-working first-year doctoral researcher at the Professorship for Algorithmic Governance and Public Policy at the Munich School of Politics and Public Policy, Technical University of Munich. I am interested in how algorithmic governance and digitalisation shape public trust, with a particular focus on the heuristics people use when making decisions. My research also explores methods and data suited to uncovering these patterns, especially conjoint experiments. Beyond this, I experiment with multimodal approaches that bring together text, audio, image, and video. I enjoy presenting my research in accessible and creative formats, ranging from presentations and articles to videos and posters.

I have grown up in Bangalore, a city in Southern India, where I pursued my Bachelor of Arts in Economics with a minor in Data, Democracy, and Development from Azim Premji University. For my master’s I traveled to Helsinki to study Social Data Science and graduated with a Master’s in Social Sciences from the University of Helsinki. At present, I am starting my doctoral research at the Munich School of Politics and Public Policy at the Technical University of Munich, in Germany.
