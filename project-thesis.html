Evaluation of Algorithmic Systems in Public Governance

Testing Methodological Cohesion in Conjoint Analysis.

Tools & Methods
Conjoint Experiments R Latent Class Analysis Marginal Means (MM) AMCE

As part of my Master's in Social Data Science at the University of Helsinki, I focused on examining public perceptions of algorithmic decision-making (ADM) systems. The core objective was to understand what drives public trust and legitimacy when governance shifts from human-led to algorithm-assisted frameworks.

A significant portion of my research was dedicated to exploring how conjoint experiments can reveal nuanced patterns in human decision-making. By setting up complex models, I compared standard estimation methods against latent class analysis to investigate attribute non-attendance.

Key Contributions:
• Designed frameworks to test public trust in ADM.
• Applied cognitive modeling to understand heuristic decision-making.
• Processed and cleaned large-scale survey data to run AMCE and MM estimations.
